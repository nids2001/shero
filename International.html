International Women's Day (IWD) is held on March 8th every year, and is a public holiday in some countries. It focuses on the movement of women's right and the 2018 IWD's theme is "Time is Now: Rural and urban activists transforming women’s lives." Below are some examples of how people around the world celebrate the Women's Day this year.

Philadelphia, USA

  • The second annual Philadelphia Women in Public Safety Conference was held at the University of Pennsylvania this year on March 8th and 9th. This two-day conference focused on how women can succeed in their public service field. Philadelphia Mayor Jim Kenney opened the event this year.
    • Learn more from the CBS News

    Shanghai, China

  • While International Women's Day is an official holiday in China, all women in China will have half day off on March 8th. Many restaurants offered free brunches, and many organizations held panel discussions and dialogues about women's rights.

    • Istanbul, Turkey

  • Istanbul also organized a series of events on March 8th this year. Events include but not limit to "Art & Dine Sculpture Workshop at Swissotel", "World Women's Day: Song Recital by Maria Gessler", and "Circolo Roma Holiday Lunch"
    • Click here to learn more

    Miami, USA

  • In Miami, many organizations also held events on International Women's Day. Organizations "Moms Demand Action, Florida" organized a march on this day and many other organizations held panel discussions and conference on March Women's Day.
    • Click here to learn more from NY Times

    India

  • Millions of women gathered across the world to strike, protest and party to mark International Women’s Day on Thursday, and India women were not exception. In India, women marched in several cities including Delhi, Karachi and Kolkata.
